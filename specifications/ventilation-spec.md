# Ventilation Specification

## General Requirements

Each sauna requires independent supply and exhaust airflow paths.

---

## Electric Sauna (Traditional)

### Supply Air
- **Location:** Low, near heater (within 300mm of floor)
- **Size:** 100–150mm diameter or equivalent
- **Type:** Adjustable louver or sliding vent

### Exhaust Air
- **Location:** High, opposite wall from heater
- **Size:** 100–150mm diameter or equivalent
- **Type:** Adjustable louver

### Notes
- Heater requires fresh air for combustion and convection
- Vent paths must be heat-rated to 100°C+
- Do not dump moist air into hidden wall cavities

---

## Infrared Sauna

### Supply Air
- **Location:** Low, near floor
- **Size:** 100mm diameter or equivalent

### Exhaust Air
- **Location:** High, opposite wall from supply
- **Size:** 100mm diameter or equivalent

---

## Vestibule

### Exhaust
- Gentle exhaust ventilation to prevent humidity buildup
- Critical near pool-facing window to prevent condensation
- Consider small exhaust fan or passive vent to exterior

---

## Materials

- All ducts/vents in sauna zones: heat-rated metal
- Dampers: stainless steel or high-temp rated
- Grilles: wood or powder-coated metal matching interior finish
