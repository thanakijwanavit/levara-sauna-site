# Electrical Specification

## Circuit Requirements

| System | Power | Circuit | Notes |
|--------|-------|---------|-------|
| Electric Sauna Heater | 8–10 kW | Dedicated 3-phase or high-amperage single-phase | Per manufacturer requirements |
| Infrared Panels | 2–3.5 kW | Dedicated circuit | Per manufacturer requirements |
| Lighting (Saunas) | 200W max | Shared with vestibule or dedicated | LED, sauna-rated |
| Lighting (Vestibule) | 300W max | General lighting circuit | |
| Controls | Low voltage | Control circuit | |

## Equipment Location

### Inside Hot Zones
- Heater/IR panels only
- Sauna-rated wiring (silicone insulated, heat-resistant)
- Minimize junction boxes inside sauna

### Outside Hot Zones (Vestibule or Service Void)
- Drivers and transformers
- Control units
- Main disconnects
- Timers

## Control Panel Requirements

Located in vestibule, accessible before entering saunas:

- Temperature setpoint (digital or analog)
- Timer with maximum runtime limit
- Safety overheat cutoff (automatic)
- Interior temperature display
- On/Off indicator lights

## Safety

- Interior temperature sensor in electric sauna (mounted at head height)
- Overheat protection to cut power at 105°C
- All sauna electrical work per local codes (IP ratings, heat ratings)
- GFCI/RCD protection for all circuits

## Lighting Specification

### Saunas
- Indirect LED placement (behind backrest, under bench)
- Color temperature: 2700–3000K (warm)
- All components sauna-rated (IP44 minimum, 100°C+ rated)
- Dimmable preferred

### Vestibule
- Warm ambient lighting
- Optional accent strip to frame pool view
- Standard indoor ratings acceptable
